Vestigial from early days. The deploy branch in cmd/strike writes attestation.json
plus three .sigstore.json bundles into an os.MkdirTemp dir that is defer-removed;
nothing reads them between write and delete. The durable paths are independent: the
signed bundles are attached as OCI referrers from att.Signed.*.Bundle in memory via
registry.AttachStatementBundles, and the recorded attestation digest comes from the
compact marshal in recordAttestation. So the disk emit is pure write-then-delete and
contradicts host-neutrality (ADR-026: containers are the only storage, nothing on the
controller host, RO-capable).

Removal also moots the item-0060 question of whether Attestation.JSON() (MarshalIndent)
needed canonicalizing: it is deleted outright. writeToOutputDir then loses all callers
and is deleted; Attestation.JSON() keeps only test callers and is deleted with its two
test usages adjusted. The pack-branch outDir (os.OpenRoot input staging) and
removeStrikeScratch retain legitimate callers and must stay.

Sequence: independent of item-0060; can land before or after. Landing before trims
0060's surface (one fewer marshal site to reason about). Open at authoring time: confirm
no other consumer of the written files exists module-wide (test/ included) before deletion.

## Final summary

Removed MkdirTemp/writeToOutputDir/defer block from executeDeploy. Deleted writeToOutputDir and its tests. Deleted Attestation.JSON() and adjusted two deploy_test.go callers to json.Marshal. Pack-step outDir and removeStrikeScratch retained. make check green, deadcode clean.
