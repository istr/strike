goal: "The lane declares both an IP (connection endpoint) and a hostname (verification anchor) for the DoT resolver, and strike verifies the resolver certificate's SAN/CN against the declared hostname in addition to the existing fingerprint/CA-bundle pin"
acceptance_intent: "The lane resolver declaration accepts an IP plus a hostname; strike connects to the declared IP, sends SNI for the declared hostname, and verifies the certificate SAN/CN against it on top of (not instead of) the fingerprint or CA-bundle check; once the resolver is reachable strike performs a one-shot cross-check (resolve the declared hostname through the resolver and confirm the result includes the declared IP), aborting the lane on mismatch"

Originated as ADR-028 roadmap decision D14 (the ADR-028 roadmap is retired). Hardening property: closes the wrong-IP-for-right-hostname failure mode; basic functionality works without it (fingerprint pinning already rejects wrong-IP-different-cert). Brings the resolver declaration in line with how DoT is configured elsewhere (Android Private DNS, systemd-resolved, OpenWrt: IP plus hostname) and makes the lane self-validating against the resolver's DNS view. Documented as 'Future direction' in docs/DNS-RESOLVER-CONFIGURATION.md.
