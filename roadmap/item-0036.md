Drift-invariant notes:

This is the culmination of the spec-layering arc, which began as a critique of
the CUE structure. The arc's earlier items cleaned the scalar typing (item-0032)
and the Go-side type surface (item-0034); this item fixes the structural root:
the mapping from CUE packages to Go packages.

Diagnosis (ratified 2026-06-25): almost everything lives in CUE package lane and
is generated into internal/lane (services tier), regardless of whether it is
foundational vocabulary (base-scalars, base-transport, base-peer, base-provenance,
base-target) or a high-tier wire/api consumer (api-*, wire-*). Because vocabulary
and consumer share one package, references are intra-package -- no import, @go()
targets intact -- which is exactly why it "just works" today and exactly why the
vocabulary is pinned to a high tier. That single co-location is the structural
root: it forces the transport -> lane direction (lane's peer/wire types embed
transport vocabulary while transport sits below lane) and every cross-tier string
boundary item-0034 has to hoist (transport cannot reference lane.Identifier). The
single-package-lane export is legacy, pre-clean-up, not a chosen design.

The invariant this item establishes: a CUE package maps to one Go package at the
layer its content belongs to, and the CUE import DAG is the arch-lint tier DAG.
Vocabulary at the bottom (foundation), consumers above, dependencies downward
only. Once that holds, the CUE setup is close to final -- the mapping is principled
and stable rather than a historical accident, and the cross-tier cycles cannot
recur by construction.

Target structure (refine the exact package names and Go home at execution): the
base-* vocabulary becomes its own CUE package generating into a foundation-tier Go
package (the natural home for the contract's vocabulary, depending only on the
contract layer); transport, lane, mediator, and the rest import it downward;
lane keeps its DAG/State logic and the api/wire generated types but sources the
scalar and structural vocabulary cross-package. attest, crossval, and trustlayers
are already separate CUE packages and are confirmed at their correct layers, not
moved.

Load-bearing spike, before any byte-exact instruction (the same unknown item-0033
isolated, now central to the whole restructure): whether a gengotypes-generated
consumer (api-artifact.cue, wire-lane.cue) can import the new vocabulary package
and still emit its own field @go() targets correctly. ADR-047 (3) recorded that
adding an import to a CUE file makes that file's @go() package target be ignored;
the attest package proves cross-package references work only for a hand-written
consumer, and the generated-consumer case is unverified, with no evidence the
gengotypes behaviour has changed. Measure in a throwaway worktree, do not guess.
If a generated consumer loses its @go() targets on import, the fallback is
hand-written consumers (as attest does) or the vocabulary stays in lane and
ADR-047 (3)/(6) stands; the restructure does not land in its generated form.

Relationship to other items:
- Supersedes item-0033: the transport CUE package is one slice of this carve-out,
  not a separate move. Set item-0033 to superseded once this is filed (executor
  lane).
- Unblocks item-0034's cross-package dimension: after this lands, the only
  remaining type-cleanliness work is the intra-lane Identifier/DAG/State cascade
  and the per-family widenings, all free of cycle-forced boundaries. Sequence this
  before item-0034's cross-package families (Host, the transport.New boundary).

This item revisits ADR-047 (3)/(6), which deliberately kept the generated layers
in one CUE package precisely to avoid the @go()-on-import risk; the revisit is
warranted only if the spike clears it, and the amendment is an append-only edit
made at execution time, never here. The byte-exact instructions are authored
ephemerally against the then-current pin; nothing byte-exact is stored here.
