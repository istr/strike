Fork D is ratified and its infrastructure has landed, so the layer-name maps
(R-24) are unblocked. The enum policy is: a top-level string-disjunction
definition generates a named Go type, and the genenums pass emits its values as
unexported typed constants beside the generated types; the vocabulary is never a
public call-site literal, so consumers reach it through a method owned by the
type's package; union discriminator tags stay boundary literals and are out of
scope. The exhaustive linter is wired and checks any switch over such a type.
The blueprint is the deploy strategy enum (commits c40ebe9, aa1a9a1). Applying
the policy to the layer names requires hoisting that vocabulary to a top-level
definition first, which is a confirmation-gated CUE change.
